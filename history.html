The first day involves the drawing of the lots when the horses are assigned to each district. The districts have their own jockey but not their own horse.

Each districts also have its own museum showcasing memorabilia and its past victories, not to mention the many churches where each horse is blessed on the day of the race

Caterpillar, Dragon, Eagle, Forest, Giraffe, Goose, Owl, Panther, Porcupine, Ram, She-Wolf, Seashell, Snail, Tortoise, Tower, Unicorn and Wave are the 17 districts and each features its own emblem and colors

Only 10 out of the 17 districts take part in each race (10 on July 2 and 10 on August 16)

The 7 that did not participate in the previous race will run in the next one, while the remaining three are drawn at random. This means that a contrada may be running both, one or no races at all.

The race only lasts about a minute, as the horses must complete three full laps around the square the first horse that crosses the finish line after the third lap wins.

The horse can also finish without the jockey. This race is rather dangerous, especially in certain places on the square and accidents have occurred in the past.
