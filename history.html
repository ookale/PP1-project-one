Palio di Siena

The Palio is a huge tradition that lasts a total of four days, from the warm-ups on the morning of June 29 until the races on July 2 and again from August 13-16 for the next Palio. The first day involves the drawing of the lots and when the horses are assigned to each district.

The districts have their own jockey but not their own horse. The city hosts 6 trial runs before the official race, one in the morning and one in the afternoon, but jockeys try to avoid tiring their horses too much.

There is fierce competition and rivalry between the various districts and the prestige of winning the Palio is of great importance to the inhabitants of each district. There are 17 different and only 10 out of the 17 take part in each race (10 on July 2 and 10 on August 16)

The 7 that did not participate in the previous race will run in the next one, while the remaining three are drawn at random. This means that a contrada may be running both, one or no races at all.
